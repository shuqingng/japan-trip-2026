Your 7-Day Journey

Tokyo (Days 1–3 & 7) → Kawaguchiko (Days 4–6) — curated for mixed mobility, nature lovers, and quieter experiences.

Essentials & Tips

Everything the family needs to know before and during the trip.

📅 Book These in Advance

  • teamLab Planets — sells out weeks ahead; book a weekday slot
  • Hamarikyu Teahouse — Nakajima teahouse matcha reservation recommended
  • Highway bus — Willer Express or Fujikyu; reserve seats early
  • Kawaguchiko hotel — lakeside rooms fill fast for late April
  • Rental car — pick up at Kawaguchiko Station; international permit required

⚠️ Closed Days to Note

  • Shinjuku Gyoen — closed Mondays
  • Kubota Itchiku Art Museum — closed Tuesdays
  • Kawaguchiko Music Forest — closed Tuesdays & Wednesdays
  • Plan Kawaguchiko museum days on Thursday–Sunday to be safe

👵 For Auntie's Comfort

  • Best spots: Oishi Park, Oshino Hakkai, Music Forest, sightseeing boat
  • Water bus (Day 2) is fully accessible — everyone sits and sightseeing from the boat
  • The rental car in Kawaguchiko means door-to-door convenience everywhere
  • Bring a lightweight portable chair or walking stick as backup
  • Skip Chureito Pagoda stairs — enjoy the base area instead

🌸 Late April in Japan

  • Temperature: 15–22°C — bring a light layer
  • Rain: Light showers possible; pack a compact umbrella each
  • Fuji views: Clearest before 11am — prioritise morning viewpoints
  • Flowers: Wisteria peaks in late April; fresh green foliage everywhere
  • Post-cherry blossom = fewer tourists at most spots

🚌 Getting Around

  • IC Card: Get Suica or Pasmo at the airport — use for trains, buses, convenience stores
  • Tokyo: Taxis for your aunt where train access is difficult; most stations have lifts
  • Water bus: Hamarikyu → Asakusa takes 40 min, fully scenic
  • Kawaguchiko: Rental car is essential — gives full flexibility
  • Highway bus: Shinjuku Bus Terminal → Kawaguchiko (~2 hrs)

🍜 Food to Try

  • Hōtō noodles — Yamanashi's thick miso noodle soup; essential in Kawaguchiko
  • Soba — especially at Oshino Hakkai's thatched-roof restaurants
  • Matcha & wagashi — at Hamarikyu's Nakajima Teahouse
  • Tempura — classic Tokyo dinner in Asakusa's backstreets
  • Lavender ice cream — at Oishi Park's lakeside café
